Mark Twain's classic message about boy adventures is one of the favourite nineteenth-century American novels. There is no shortage of fun, sincerity, energy and interesting character, and it is a vivid portrayal of small-town life along the Mississippi River. Tom Sawyer is an unusual boy – naughty, enthusiastic and resourceful. Trouble and adventures accompany him every step. Along with his friend Huckleberry Finn, Tom escapes home to become a pirate. In this story Tom Sawyer meets his first real love, becomes a witness to murder, and works out a lot of misdeeds.

Joe Logan’s brother Brendan is with the Provos. Mr logan thinks Brendan a hero, fighting as he had for the Republic; but Joe resents his stupid and selfish brother. When Brendan intrudes on Joe’s friendship with Kathleen Doherty jealousy makes him bitter. Joe’s reaction is only normal, but against the bleack background of hatred and conflict in Derry, his words have horrific consequences. Love and hate have a different complexion in Northern Ireland.
